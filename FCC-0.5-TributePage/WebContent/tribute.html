Paramahansa Yogananda

The Man Who Brought Yoga to the West

Here's a timeline of Yogananda's life:

  • 1893 - Born Mukunda Lal Ghosh in Gorakpur, India
  • 1904 - Moves to Barielly, India. His mother dies in Calcutta while making preparations for the wedding of his eldest brother, Ananta
  • 1906 - His family relocates to Calcutta, India
  • 1910 - Meets his guru, Swami Sri Yukteswar Giri, and begins college studes in Calcutta
  • 1915 - Receives his university degree, and is initiated into the Swami Order by Sri Yukteswar
  • 1920 - With the encouragement of his guru, travels to America to speak at a religious conference in Boston, MA
  • 1925 - He organizes his teaching mission under the name of "Self-Realization Fellowship," with headquarters on Mt. Washington in Los Angeles, CA
  • 1935 - Travels to India via the U.K. and Western Europe at the request of his guru, who then makes his transition in 1936. Yogananda returns to America after the death of Yukteswar.
  • 1947 - Publishes Autobiography of a Yogi, one of the most famous autobiographical books on yoga, which has sold more than 4 million copies
  • 1952 - Makes his transition in Los Angeles, CA

"The value of Yogananda's Autobiography is greatly enhanced by the fact that it is one of the few books in English about the wise men of India which has been written, not by a journalist or foreigner, but by one of their own race and training -- in short, a book about yogis by a yogi."

- W.Y. Evans-Wentz, M.A., D.Litt., D.Sc., Jesus College, Oxford
